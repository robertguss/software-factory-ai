# Design decisions

Conveyor's architecture is shaped by 22 accepted ADRs in `docs/adrs/`. Each ADR
is numbered, decision-oriented, and tied to a gated milestone in the Phase 1.5/2
program. This page summarizes all 22 with a one-line summary and a pointer to
the full ADR.

ADRs override `docs/BRAINSTORM.md` when the two conflict. The brainstorm is
living strategy; the ADRs are durable decisions. When contract semantics change,
update the relevant ADR, schema, and phase doc together so reviewers do not have
to reconcile drift.

## Themes

A few themes run through the ADRs and are worth calling out:

- **Determinism boundary.** The BEAM conductor owns state, policy, evidence, and
  gate verdicts; agents own drafting, implementation, and judgment. ADRs 06, 07,
  14, and 15 all reinforce this.
- **Immutability with lifecycle.** Evidence is immutable, but immutability does
  not mean infinite retention. ADRs 04, 09, 10, and 20 separate immutable
  identity from retention, redaction, and evolution.
- **Actor separation.** The agent that writes code must not author its own
  acceptance contract or red-team tests. ADRs 07, 13, and 19 enforce this.
- **Fail closed.** When in doubt, block. ADRs 02, 06, 11, 13, and 22 all prefer
  false positives over silent bypass.
- **Projections are not authority.** UI, CLI, and static reports derive from
  canonical artifacts. ADR 21 makes this explicit and testable.
